Mega Food Drives

In India, a lot of people face the problem of hunger everyday, sometimes people don't have enough money to afford even a single a day.

Recently India was ranked at 101 out of 116 on Global Hunger Index. Nearly 40 per cent of the food produced in India is wasted every year due to fragmented food systems and inefficient supply chains â€” a figure estimated by the Food and Agricultural Organisation (FAO). To contribute our bit for the society we aim to organise food drives.

We have successfully organised four mega food drives feeding more than 200 people under our project #EndHunger and No Hunger India and we feel it's never a bad time to help those who are in need. Our aim is to organise more frequent mega food drives and huge campaigns to fight hunger and ensure that these children don't sleep empty stomach.

During a pandemic when people lost their jobs and workers who rely on a per day salary to feed their family faced problems and inconvenience and it started with helping them and we are expanding our reach, Academic drive is also complemented with distribution of eatable items such as fruit,fast food.

Food is prepared, packed and distributed with love by our volunteers. More than 30 volunteers participated in food drives and Day by Day we are growing and aiming to feed more people.It started with bringing out food for NGO students and we have gradually expanded it to other needy people.

Teamwork helps to achieve goals and together we can, Maggi distribution drive wherein every teacher cooked two maggi each and we all enjoyed it with children without putting burden on a single person. Anyone who wishes to distribute food can contact us and team facilitates them. Volunteers love to celebrate their special days with these children. Volunteers celebrate their birthdays by distributing food to the students.

We collaborated with a few cafes to distribute food and wish to create awareness with their help to reduce food wastage.
